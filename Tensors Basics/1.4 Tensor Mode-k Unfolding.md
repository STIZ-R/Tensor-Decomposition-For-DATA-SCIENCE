## 1.4 Tensor Mode-*k* Unfloding

We can rearrange a tensor to form multiple matrices. The process is called **unfolding** or also known as **matricization**.

<br>

> **Definition (Informal Definition of Mode-*k* Unfolding)** <br>
> The **mode-*k* unfolding** of a tensor is a matrix whose columns are the mode-*k* fibers of that tensor, denoted as $\textbf{X}_{(k)}$.

<br>

Let take a 3-way tensor of size $2 \times 2 \times 2$. Hera are the different mode-*k* unfolding.

> **Figure 2:** Front and Back slices of X

```math
X(:,:,1) =
\begin{bmatrix}
8 & 2 \\
9 & 9
\end{bmatrix}, \quad
X(:,:,2) =
\begin{bmatrix}
6 & 3 \\
1 & 5
\end{bmatrix}
```

```math
\text{a) Mode-1 unfolding}
\begin{bmatrix}
8 & 2 & 6 & 3 \\
9 & 9 & 1 & 5
\end{bmatrix}
```

```math
\text{b) Mode-2 unfolding}
\begin{bmatrix}
8 & 9 & 6 & 1 \\
2 & 9 & 3 & 5
\end{bmatrix}
```

```math
\text{c) Mode-3 unfolding}
\begin{bmatrix}
8 & 9 & 2 & 9 \\
6 & 1 & 3 & 5
\end{bmatrix}
```






## Exercises

<br>

> **Exercise 1.12** <br>
> Let $\textbf{X}$ be a tensor of size $10 \times 8 \times 6$. <br>
> What is the size of $\textbf{X}_{(2)}$?

### Solution - Exercise 1.12

The size of $\textbf{X}_{(2)}$ is a matrix of $8$ rows and $10 \times 6$ columns or simply $10 \times 8 \times 6 = 480$ elements.
