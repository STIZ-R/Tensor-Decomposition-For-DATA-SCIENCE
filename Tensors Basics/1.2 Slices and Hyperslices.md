### 1.2 - Slices and Hyperslices

> **Definition (Slices of 3-way Tensor)**  
> Let $\mathbf{X}$ be a 3-way tensor of size $m \times n \times p$.  
> The $i$-th **horizontal slice** is a matrix of size $n \times p$ given by $\mathbf{X}(i,:,:)$.  
> The $j$-th **lateral slice** is a matrix of size $m \times p$ given by $\mathbf{X}(:,j,:)$.  
> The $k$-th **frontal slice** is a matrix of size $m \times n$ given by $\mathbf{X}(:,:,k)$.

```text
  +--------------+        +--------------+          +--------------+
 /              /|       /  /  /  /  /  /|        /--------------/|
+-------------+ /|      +-------------+  |        +-------------+ ||
|_____________|//|      | |  |  |  |  |  |        |             | ||
|_____________|//|      | |  |  |  |  |  |        |             | ||
|_____________|//+      | |  |  |  |  |  +        |             | |+
|_____________|//       | |  |  |  |  | /         |             |/
+-------------+         +-------------+           +-------------+
a) Horizontal slices, X(i,:,:)   b) Lateral slices, X(:,j,:)   c) Frontal slices, X(:,:,k)
```

>**Exercise 1.3**
>For the tensor in Figure 1:
>a) What is X(1,:,:)?
>b) What is X(3,:,:)?
>c) What is X(:,1,:)?
>d) What is X(:,2,:)?

```text
Front slice: X(:,:,1)          Back slice: X(:,:,2)

  +---------+                    +---------+
  | 3  9  1 |                    | 6  9  5 |
  | 8  2  1 |                    | 5  6  4 |
  | 4  3  9 |                    | 1  4  1 |
  +---------+                    +---------+
				 a) Figure 1

```

<details>
<summary>Show answers</summary>

$\mathbf{X}(1,:,:) =$  
\[
\begin{bmatrix}
3 & 9 & 1 \\
8 & 2 & 1 \\
4 & 3 & 9
\end{bmatrix}
\]

$\mathbf{X}(3,:,:) =$  
\[
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 4 & 1 \\
5 & 6 & 4 \\
6 & 9 & 5
\end{bmatrix}
\]

$\mathbf{X}(:,1,:) =$  
\[
\begin{bmatrix}
3 & 6 \\
8 & 5 \\
4 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
\]

$\mathbf{X}(:,2,:) =$  
\[
\begin{bmatrix}
9 & 9 \\
2 & 6 \\
3 & 4
\end{bmatrix}
\]

</details>
